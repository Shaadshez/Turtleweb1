Discover the Wonders of Sri Lanka's Turtle Populations

Sri Lanka is home to five out of the seven species of sea turtles in the world, including the Green Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. These magnificent creatures are an important part of the country's coastal ecosystem, and have been an integral part of Sri Lankan culture and folklore for centuries.

Join us as we explore the fascinating lives and conservation efforts of Sri Lanka's turtle populations. Learn about the different species of turtles and their habitats, the threats they face, and the important work being done to protect them. Discover how you can get involved and make a difference in the conservation of these amazing animals.





Protecting Sri Lanka's Sea Turtles: A Conservation Journey

One of Sri Lanka's attractive features is its growing turtle population, which contributes to the island nation's reputation for having a rich biodiversity and different ecosystems. Sri Lanka offers a sanctuary for numerous kinds of sea turtles along its beautiful coastline, making it a paradise for both turtle enthusiasts and environment lovers. Five of the seven sea turtle species recognized worldwide are found in the island nation, highlighting its importance as a global hub for turtle conservation.Visitors will come across a variety of unique species when researching Sri Lanka's turtle population, including the Green Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. These amazing animals and their habitats are the focus of Sri Lanka's conservation efforts. By engaging in responsible tourism practices, tourists can play a significant part in preserving Sri Lanka's turtle species for future generations.
