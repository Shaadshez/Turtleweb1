Categories of Turtles in Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa, a beautiful coastal town in Sri Lanka, is home to several species of sea turtles. These include the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle.

Each of these turtle species has unique characteristics and behaviors. The Green Turtle, for example, is named for the green color of its fat and is known for its migratory behavior. The Loggerhead Turtle is the largest hard-shelled turtle in the world and is named for its large head. The Olive Ridley Turtle is the smallest of the sea turtles and is named for the greenish color of its shell. The Hawksbill Turtle has a pointed beak and is known for its beautiful shell, which is used to make jewelry and other decorative items. The Leatherback Turtle is the largest turtle in the world and has a unique soft and rubbery shell.

These turtles play an important role in maintaining the balance of the marine ecosystem and are a popular tourist attraction in Hikkaduwa. At the Hikkaduwa Hatchery, visitors can learn about these fascinating creatures and even participate in conservation efforts to protect their nesting sites.






Leatherback Turtles

Leatherback turtles are the largest of all sea turtles and are known for their unique shell, which is covered in a leathery, rubbery skin rather than a hard shell. Hikkaduwa beach in Sri Lanka is a well-known nesting site for leatherback turtles, and many conservation efforts are underway to protect these magnificent creatures. Leatherback turtles face many threats, including habitat destruction, pollution, and accidental capture in fishing gear.




Hawksbill Turtles

These turtles are a popular attraction for visitors to the beach, as they can often be spotted swimming in the clear turquoise waters or basking on the sandy shore. Hikkaduwa Beach is home to a marine sanctuary, established in 2002 to protect the coral reefs and the many species that rely on them, including hawksbill turtles.




Green Turtles

These turtles are known for their distinctive greenish-colored skin and their relatively large size, with adults typically weighing between 200 and 500 pounds. Green turtles are herbivores, feeding primarily on seagrass and algae, and are an important part of the marine ecosystem




Loggerhead Turtles

Loggerhead turtles are a species of sea turtle that can be found in many parts of the world, including the waters around Hikkaduwa beach in Sri Lanka. They are named for their large, blocky heads, which are perfectly adapted for crushing the hard shells of their favorite prey, crabs and other crustaceans. Loggerheads can grow to be quite large, with adults weighing up to 250 pounds or more.




Olive Ridely Turtles

Olive Ridley turtles are a species of sea turtle that are known for their distinctive heart-shaped shells and olive green coloring. Hikkaduwa Beach, located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, is one of the major nesting sites for Olive Ridley turtles in the region. The turtles come to the beach between October and April each year to lay their eggs in the sand.
