Kosgoda Hatchery

The Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project is a hatchery and rehabilitation center for sea turtles in Sri Lanka. The project aims to protect sea turtles and their eggs from poachers and natural predators, and release them back into the ocean once they are strong enough to survive on their own.
The Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project is not only a hatchery and rehabilitation center for sea turtles, but it is also involved in a variety of research and conservation activities. The project conducts studies on sea turtle behavior, habitat, and nesting patterns, and actively works to reduce human impact on the local environment through community education and outreach programs.





Visiting Kosgoda Hatchery

Kosgoda Hatchery is open to visitors and offers guided tours of their facilities. Visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles and see the hatchlings up close. Visitors to Kosgoda Hatchery can take guided tours of the facilities to learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka and the conservation efforts being undertaken to protect them. The hatchlings are kept in tanks until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Visitors can see the hatchlings up close and even participate in the release of baby turtles into the sea. The hatchery also conducts research on sea turtles and their habitats, as well as outreach programs to local communities to promote sea turtle conservation. By visiting Kosgoda Hatchery, visitors can learn about the important work being done to protect these beautiful creatures and contribute to their conservation efforts.

Turtle Conservation

Kosgoda Hatchery has successfully released thousands of sea turtle hatchlings into the ocean. Their conservation efforts are aimed at protecting these endangered species and increasing their numbers. The hatchery also provides education to visitors about the importance of sea turtle conservation, including information about threats to their survival such as pollution, poaching, and habitat destruction.
